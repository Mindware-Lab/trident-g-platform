# Trident-G summary

## 1. Structural architecture

This is the Trident-G form itself.

**Shaft = Gc**
The banked, reusable support structure: validated scripts, operator chains, compressed gist, and crystallised competence.

**Centre tine = Gf**
The live relational bridge: effortful, flexible reasoning that keeps task-relevant structure active long enough to be mapped, transformed, compared, tested, compressed, and committed.

**Outer tines = the two dominant higher-engagement branches**

* **explore** / entropy-widening
* **exploit** / mutual-information-tightening

The centre tine is best understood as the live relational bridge in three linked senses: between viability control and banked Gc, between the explore and exploit branches, and between live flexible reasoning and the working-memory / attention-control substrate that keeps relational structure online long enough to be used.  

## 1A. What **G** means

**G** in Trident-G should not be reduced to **Gf** alone.

A clean definition is:

> **G is the overall usable general-intelligence range of the Trident system: the capacity to stay viable, move adaptively across the explore–exploit range, and coordinate banked Gc with live Gf so that cognition can shift from low-cost automated competence to high-demand restructuring under challenge, then crystallise what survives back into reusable Gc.**

So:

* **Gc-dominant cognition** is the more automated, lower-cost end of the range: installed scripts, validated concepts, fast retrieval, and reliable execution.
* **Gf-dominant cognition** is the higher-challenge, higher-free-energy end of the range: live relational restructuring, flexible reasoning, probing, reframing, and adaptive model revision under novelty.
* **G**, more broadly, is the capacity to operate across that whole viable range rather than at only one pole.

In that sense, Trident-G treats intelligence not only as reasoning power in the narrow psychometric sense, but as a **viable dynamic range of cognition**: the ability to preserve good function from relatively automatic Gc-led performance through to demanding Gf-led restructuring, without losing corridor control. The two highest-level meta-missions clarify this further: **Viability** protects clean operation, while **Expansion** enlarges the range and the portable repertoire that can later be banked into Gc.  

## 2. Highest-level meta-missions

These sit above the applied layers and explain what the whole Trident is for.

**Meta-mission 1: Viability**
Maintain adaptive allostasis within the defended near-critical Ψ corridor so learning, inference, and action remain clean and recoverable.

**Meta-mission 2: Expansion**
Enlarge viable dynamic range and portable operator repertoire through exploration, exploitation, gist extraction, and crystallisation into reusable Gc.

These meta-missions should be treated not only as architectural statements, but as **trainable global orientations**. Viability is the cultivated preference for clean, recoverable in-band functioning. Expansion is the cultivated preference for enlarging live reasoning range and banked portable competence rather than merely securing local wrapper-bound success.

## 4. Core applied distinctions

For programme design, it is useful to distinguish four applied layers, while remembering that they sit beneath the two meta-missions.

**Meta-missions**
What the whole system is trying to become.

**Mission frames**
What the loop is being run for right now.

**Control missions**
What keeps the loop viable, clean, and repeatable under real conditions.

**Meta-dials**
What slower governance variables change the geometry, controllability, learnability, proof discipline, scale fit, and ecology of cognition over time.

**Feedback signals**
What fast diagnostic information is telling the loop to simplify, probe, recover, update, or restructure.

These layers sit within one shared G-Loop kernel rather than forming separate theories. The shared kernel not only supports viable inference and action. It also progressively extracts gist, selects deeper invariants, and compiles what survives into reusable Gc.

## 6. Stack interpretation

This stack should not be read as a set of isolated modules. The layers are nested and mutually dependent.

Mission frames specify what success means.
The language of thought and action specifies the formal move-language by which the G-Loop is executed concretely: operator algebra supplies the reusable mindware moves, and mission grammar supplies the well-formed sequencing rules for a given frame.
The reasoning calculus specifies how inference transforms maps, claims, options, commitments, and candidate gists within those sequences.
The semantic layer specifies what the system is modelling.
The syntax layer specifies how the same deeper structure is rendered at the surface.
The subjective interface specifies how significance, cost, possibility, threat, effort, and compression success are lived by the organism.
The allostatic layer specifies whether the system is in a viable regime to reason cleanly.
The cognitive runtime specifies what can actually be held, transformed, and executed.
The fast relational runtime provides the live predictive bridge through which structure can be carried forward and acted upon.  

## 7. Meta-level note

Above the applied layer, Trident-G now includes a clearer **meta-level architecture**.

The two **meta-missions** are:

* **Viability**
* **Expansion**

Beneath them sits the **meta-dial layer**, which shapes how well the system runs across time and across contexts. The current canonical meta-dials are:

* meta-efficacy
* coherence
* compression depth
* complexity governance
* subjective calibration
* proof discipline
* progress–quality governance
* exploration–exploitation governance
* multi-scale governance
* ecology support

These are not extra mission frames or extra operators. They are slower, trainable governance qualities distributed over operators and visible in behavioural, subjective, and telemetry patterns across repeated loops. 

## 8. Kernel note

The G-Loop remains the core intelligence kernel running across all these layers. It is the smallest repeatable cycle that keeps cognition viable inside the defended Ψ corridor while converting successful inference into reusable competence. It runs by mapping, testing, committing, updating, compressing, and banking under mission criteria and signals. The kernel remains the same even when mission frames, syntaxes, wrappers, domains, and applied programmes change. 

The crucial clarification is that the operators collectively implement how the kernel thinks and acts, but they are not the whole kernel. The G-Loop also depends on semantics, subjectivity, allostatic viability, feedback signals, and runtime conditions. Operator algebra plus mission grammar are therefore the concrete mindware implementation layer of the kernel, not the entire architecture. 

## 9. Summary

Trident-G is best understood as a domain-general intelligence architecture organised around a shaft-and-tines structure and run by a common G-Loop kernel. The shaft is banked Gc competence, the centre tine is live Gf relational reasoning, and the outer tines are entropy-widening exploration and mutual-information-tightening control. The broader **G** of Trident-G is not just Gf in isolation, but the overall viable dynamic range of cognition across which the system can coordinate Gc-dominant automation with Gf-dominant restructuring under challenge, then crystallise what survives back into reusable Gc. Above the applied architecture sit the two meta-missions of Viability and Expansion, which should be treated as trainable global orientations. Beneath them sits a clearer meta-dial layer that shapes how well the kernel is run across time and across contexts. Formally, the system is organised as a language of thought and action, a reasoning calculus, a semantic layer, a syntax layer, a subjective interface, a Ψ-allostatic viability layer, a cognitive runtime, and a fast relational runtime. Together these layers explain how Trident-G integrates purpose, modelling, subjectivity, inference, selective compression, state control, and runtime execution into a single architecture.
