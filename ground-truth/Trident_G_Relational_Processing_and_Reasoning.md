## Relational processing and reasoning

### Overview

Relational processing and reasoning is the central bridge function of Trident-G. It is the live process that lets the system hold, compare, transform, and test relations rather than merely react to surface cues or retrieve fixed routines. In practical terms, it is what allows the loop to work with structure: what connects to what, what constrains what, what predicts what, and what matters most under current stakes. A good map is therefore both a predictive structure and a value landscape.

In structural terms, relational reasoning is best treated as the **live centre tine** or **working bridge** of Trident-G: the active bridge between corridor-controlled viability and the banked Gc library, coordinating movement between widening and tightening under current constraints. The shaft is Gc, the banked library of validated scripts, chunks, and concepts. The outer tines are the two dominant higher-engagement branches: entropy-widening exploration, where the system broadens search and loosens framing, and mutual-information-tightening control, where it narrows, discriminates, commits, and executes under task-relevant structure. Relational reasoning is the live process that moves adaptively between those branches while staying anchored to what has already been banked.

### Core definition

**Relational processing and reasoning**

**Everyday labels:** live structure sense, work it out, hold the pattern, infer what follows

**Meaning:** the ability to keep task-relevant relations active long enough to map them, transform them, compare them, test them, and derive implications from them under current constraints.

This is what makes the G-Loop more than a motivational cycle. It gives the loop something to operate on besides feelings of effort or vague ideas. It is also what makes transfer possible: the system is not only storing topics or wrappers, but learning how to preserve deeper structure across changes in syntax, surface form, wrapper, and stakes.

### Relation to the wider stack

Within the wider Trident-G architecture, relational processing and reasoning should not be treated as a separate top-level layer alongside mission frames, the language of thought and action, reasoning calculus, semantics, subjectivity, allostasis, and runtime. Rather, it is the **live bridge function** that links these layers in use. It operates over the **semantic layer** of ontology, situation map, and affordance surface. It is expressed procedurally through the **operator algebra** and sequenced by **mission grammar**. It may be rendered in different **syntaxes** such as verbal, visuospatial, or numerical form. It is shaped by the **subjective interface** because felt significance, pressure, and salience affect what is held, noticed, or distorted. And it depends on the **cognitive runtime**, especially working memory, attention, stability, interference resistance, re-entry, and viable computation under load.

For that reason, relational reasoning is best understood as the working centre of the G-Loop rather than as an extra primitive. It is the active process by which the system turns a map into implications, probes, comparisons, revisions, and justified next moves.

### The four bridge capacities

#### Relational fluid intelligence

**Everyday labels:** on-the-fly pattern thinking, live problem-solving, flexible reasoning

**Meaning:** the ability to detect, restructure, and work with relations when the situation is novel, uncertain, or changing. This is the flexible Gf function at the centre of the loop.

#### Working memory

**Everyday labels:** active scratchpad, keep-it-online space, mental holding room

**Meaning:** the temporary workspace that keeps rules, relations, constraints, and task-sets active long enough to be manipulated rather than lost to drift.

#### Attention management

**Everyday labels:** hold the thread, protect the task-set, keep the right thing in focus

**Meaning:** the ability to stabilise what matters, suppress misleading pulls, and stop the current loop from being hijacked by distraction, interruption, emotion, or salience spikes. This is why drift matters so much in Trident-G: when the task-set decays, relational reasoning collapses.

#### Relational inference and updating capacity

**Everyday labels:** work out what follows, derive the next link, test the implications, revise the model

**Meaning:** the ability to infer consequences from the current structure, compare competing maps, discriminate between rival explanations, and update the model when the world behaves differently from expectation.

Together, these four capacities form the bridge from the **cognitive runtime / capacity substrate** to the **mindware layer**. Runtime makes the operator algebra runnable under load. Mindware supplies reusable operators, scripts, and procedures. Relational processing is the live bridge that binds, transforms, tests, and updates structure so that mindware can be instantiated, revised, and eventually banked as validated Gc.

### Cognitive runtime, operator algebra, and Gc

#### Cognitive runtime / capacity substrate

**Everyday labels:** thinking bandwidth, control stability, low-cost cognition

**Meaning:** the part of the system that makes cognition stable, flexible, and cheap enough to run cleanly under load. It includes working memory, attention, control stability, interference resistance, re-entry quality, and viable computation under load. In Trident-G, a fast SR-like predictive relational bridge can also be treated as a computational middle layer within this runtime.

#### Mindware layer / operator algebra

**Everyday labels:** playbooks, operator scripts, reusable methods

**Meaning:** the part of the system that supplies primitive moves, procedures, policies, and operator chains. Mindware does not replace reasoning. It provides reusable structure that relational reasoning can instantiate, test, revise, and eventually bank. In stack terms, this is the **operator algebra** within the wider **language of thought and action**.

#### Gc

**Everyday labels:** installed skill, validated operator library, stored know-how

**Meaning:** the growing repertoire of stable, retrievable scripts and concepts that can be deployed without rebuilding from scratch. In Trident-G, Gc is not just memory. It is validated memory: what has survived syntax swaps, wrapper swaps, stake swaps, and boundary checks strongly enough to be trusted as portable competence.

### Where relational reasoning sits in the operator algebra

Relational reasoning is not a separate extra operator. It is the main bridge function running through several operators. The operators are rule-like mindware moves that act over relational representations in working memory. Relational reasoning is the live process that makes those operators useful.

It is especially central in:

- **Y4 Map** — building explicit relational structure
- **Y6 Unpack** — reopening a chunk to inspect hidden dependencies
- **Y7 Analogise** — transferring structure by role rather than surface resemblance
- **Y8 Abstract** — extracting deeper invariants
- **Y9 Instantiate** — binding a schema to a concrete case
- **Y10 Re-represent** — changing frame, level, or surface representation while preserving structure
- **Y11 Meta-map** — mapping relations between maps or frameworks
- **Y12 Evaluate** — assessing whether a relational claim or causal link really holds
- **Y13 Discriminate / Probe** — using targeted probes to separate competing relational models

These are the main operators by which Gf becomes useful, and by which useful Gf discoveries can later become Gc.

Relational reasoning also depends on, but is not identical with, the surrounding non-relational control operators:

- **Y1 Orient** sets the rigour budget for how much modelling and checking should be done.
- **Y2 Recover** and **Y3 Externalise / Escalate** protect viability when clean relational work cannot be sustained.
- **Y14 Validate portability** checks whether a candidate relational structure actually travels across syntaxes, wrappers, stakes, and boundaries.
- **Y15 Satisfice / Commit** converts current relational structure into a justified next move.
- **Y16 Level control / anti-regress** keeps representational work at the right granularity and prevents unproductive oscillation or regress.

### Relation to reasoning calculus

Deductive, inductive, abductive, analogical, Bayesian, and value-of-information reasoning are best understood as **reasoning styles** layered onto operator execution, not as separate top-level operators. In other words, the operator algebra defines what move the loop is making, while the reasoning calculus defines what kind of inference is being used during that move.

Examples:

- **Deductive reasoning** often rides on **Y9 Instantiate** and **Y12 Evaluate**
- **Inductive reasoning** often rides on **Y8 Abstract**, **Y12 Evaluate**, and **Y14 Validate portability**
- **Abductive reasoning** often rides on **Y4 Map** and **Y11 Meta-map**
- **Analogical reasoning** is centred in **Y7 Analogise**
- **Bayesian or statistical updating** often rides on **Y12 Evaluate**, **Y13 Discriminate / Probe**, and the update phase of action loops
- **Value-of-information reasoning** is most explicit in **Y13 Discriminate / Probe**

This layered treatment is important because it keeps the operator set compact. Deduction, induction, abduction, analogy, Bayesian updating, and value-of-information reasoning are real and central, but they are better treated as cross-cutting reasoning styles than as additional primitives.

### Relation to syntax and far transfer

Relational processing is also what makes syntax variation useful rather than superficial. The same deeper structure can be rendered in verbal, visuospatial, or numerical syntax without changing its underlying semantic target or operator demand. A learner who can preserve performance across syntax changes is showing stronger evidence that the invariant has been abstracted and can be re-instantiated across forms.

That is why relational reasoning matters so much for far transfer. The system is not only storing one successful wrapper. It is learning how to preserve deeper structure across syntax swaps, broader wrapper swaps, stakes, and real missions. Syntax robustness may count as an earlier portability threshold, but it is not the whole of far transfer by itself.

### Relation to the G-Loop

Relational processing is what makes the G-Loop a genuine adaptive inference engine rather than a sequence of motivational slogans. It gives the loop something to operate on besides effort, mood, or habit.

In the **Map** phase, it organises the current problem into predictive structure and value gradients, mainly through **Y4 Map, Y5 Chunk, Y8 Abstract, and Y11 Meta-map**. In the **Test** phase, it compares alternatives and identifies the most diagnostic probe, mainly through **Y12 Evaluate, Y13 Discriminate / Probe, and Y14 Validate portability**. In the **Commit** phase, it translates current structure into a justified next move, mainly through **Y15 Satisfice / Commit**. In the **Bank / Install** phase, it helps determine whether a success reflects deep structure or only a syntax- or wrapper-bound trick, with **Y14 Validate portability** acting as the main portability threshold.

That is why mismatch is so important. Mismatch is not merely difficulty. It is a sign that the current map or policy is wrong, incomplete, or wrongly calibrated, and therefore that relational restructuring, probing, or recovery is needed. Acute mismatch often calls first for simplification and stabilisation. Persistent or unexpectedly unstable mismatch increases pressure for deeper restructuring.

In this sense, relational reasoning is the working centre of the G-Loop: it is what makes it possible to move productively between widening and tightening, between exploration and commitment, and between live flexible reasoning and banked portable competence.

### Subjectivity and relational reasoning

Because Trident-G treats subjectivity as the lived interface of the loop, relational reasoning never operates in a fully neutral space. Feelings, motivations, emotions, urges, and felt significance shape what is noticed, what is held in working memory, what is treated as salient, and how the current map is valued. This does not replace relational reasoning, but it does shape its conditions. Affect can raise load, alter salience, bias action competition, destabilise the task-set, or distort the current map. Conversely, distancing, reappraisal, pacing, and recovery can restore cleaner relational inference.

### Summary

Relational processing and reasoning is the central bridge function of Trident-G: it uses the cognitive runtime to hold and transform task-relevant structure online, operates through the operator algebra within the wider language of thought and action, links the semantic layer to viable action, and allows fluid Gf discoveries to become validated Gc through probing, updating, syntax and wrapper variation, portability testing, and conservative banking. It is therefore the working centre of the G-Loop rather than a separate extra primitive.
