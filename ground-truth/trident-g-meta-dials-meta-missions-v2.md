# Appendix Z. Meta-dials & Meta-missions

## Z0. Purpose

This appendix defines a clearer meta-level governance layer for Trident-G and H-AGI training.

The purpose of this layer is not to replace the G-Loop kernel, the mission framework, or the formal layer of reasoning and action. Its purpose is to provide a small set of higher-order training principles that organise how the loop is run across many operators, tactics, strategies, missions, and contexts.

This is needed because some of the most important determinants of intelligence in real environments are not single operators or single local tactics. They are slower, cross-cutting governance competences: how deeply one compresses structure, how one balances exploration against exploitation, how one balances progress against perfectionism, how well one calibrates feelings as signals, how well one controls scale, and how strongly one insists on proof before banking a script as competence.

The current Trident-G architecture already supports this move. It distinguishes meta-missions, meta-dials, mission frames, control missions, feedback signals, the formal layer of reasoning and action, and the wider semantic, subjective, allostatic, and runtime layers. Within that architecture, meta-dials are best treated as slow shapers of controllability, learnability, corridor width, ecological support, proof discipline, compression quality, and noise rather than as fast in-session missions.

A further clarification is important. These governance competences do not replace underlying bridge capacities such as working memory, relational binding capacity, attention control, or relational inference. They shape how well those capacities are recruited, stabilised, and coordinated across repeated loops and real environments.

## Z1. Design rule

A meta-dial should meet four conditions:

- It should shape the quality of many loops, not only one task.
- It should recruit multiple operators rather than reduce to a single operator.
- It should be trainable and coachable as a recognisable competence.
- It should have a meaningful subjective and behavioural signature over time.

So the meta-dial layer is best understood as the training-facing governance layer of the architecture.

The kernel still runs by mapping, testing, committing, updating, compressing, and banking. The mission framework still specifies what the loop is being run for. The formal layer still supplies the concrete structured moves. But the meta-dials shape how well, how often, how deeply, and how adaptively that whole system is run.

## Z2A. Trainable meta-mission orientation

The meta-missions of Trident-G should be treated not only as abstract architectural statements, but also as trainable global orientations.

They are not peer meta-dials. They sit above the meta-dial layer and specify the highest-level direction of development for the whole system.

In practical terms:

- **Meta-missions** define what the system is trying to become.
- **Meta-dials** define the trainable governance qualities by which it gets there.

This distinction matters because if Viability and Expansion are placed in the same list as compression depth, proof discipline, or exploration–exploitation governance, the architecture begins to blur purpose and means.

### Z2A.1 Viability orientation

**Definition**

A cultivated preference for keeping cognition clean, recoverable, and in-band rather than glorifying grind, chaos, unstable intensity, or forced output while out of corridor.

**Why it matters**

The G-Loop repeatedly states that the first question is whether the system is in a workable state to run a clean loop, and the wider protocol is explicit that out-of-band functioning contaminates learning and credit assignment. Viability is therefore not merely a background condition. It is a trainable commitment to protecting the conditions under which good inference, learning, and action remain trustworthy.

**User-facing meaning**

- protect in-zone functioning
- recover early
- do not push heavy training or decision-making while clearly out of corridor
- value clean re-entry over heroic forcing
- prefer stable repeatability to volatile intensity

**Practical indicators**

- in-band time
- re-entry speed
- drift control
- overload avoidance
- reduced bad updates under hot or flat states

### Z2A.2 Expansion orientation

**Definition**

A cultivated preference for enlarging viable dynamic range and portable operator repertoire, then crystallising what survives into reusable Gc, rather than merely repeating familiar local wins inside one wrapper.

**Why it matters**

The current Trident-G architecture already defines Expansion as enlarging viable dynamic range and portable operator repertoire through exploration, exploitation, gist extraction, and crystallisation into Gc. In training terms, this becomes a preference for growth in live reasoning range and portable competence, rather than performance theatre, local fluency, or path-dependent success.

**User-facing meaning**

- widen viable dynamic range
- build stronger live Gf for restructuring, probing, and adaptation
- convert what survives into Gc
- prefer portable operators over wrapper-bound tricks
- value transfer and banked competence over one-off polished performance

**Practical indicators**

- gist quality
- portability checks passed
- operator repertoire growth
- banked script quality
- successful deployment in new wrappers
- reliable cue-fired transfer into real use

### Z2A.3 Practical note

The trainable meaning of the meta-missions is not simply “wanting” them in a vague sense. It is learning to:

- value in-band functioning over false progress
- notice when one is sacrificing viability for output theatre
- value portable growth over local performance display
- choose loops, habits, and training pressures that build Gf-to-Gc conversion rather than merely generating fluent surface success

So the meta-missions should be treated as global coaching principles, identity-level training aims, and selection pressures on the whole programme, while the meta-dials remain the main trainable mechanisms underneath them.

## Z3. Canonical meta-dials

### Z3.1 Meta-efficacy | Earned agency confidence

**Definition**

Grounded confidence that one can engage, recover, update, and act effectively under challenge.

**Why it matters**

If this dial is too low, good operators are not run consistently enough to matter. Avoidance, hesitation, and failed re-entry degrade the whole loop before reasoning quality can even be expressed.

**Subjective face**

Resolve, willingness to engage, reduced helplessness, stronger re-entry after setbacks.

**Main operator anchors**

- Y1 Orient
- Y2 Recover
- Y3 Externalise / Escalate
- Y15 Satisfice / Commit

**Typical failure modes**

- avoidance
- deferral
- over-bracing
- learned hesitation
- giving up too early under mismatch

### Z3.2 Coherence | Internal consistency and signal reliability

**Definition**

The degree to which goals, definitions, success criteria, steps, and checks hang together.

**Why it matters**

Coherence lowers nuisance noise and improves monitoring quality. It determines whether the person is even running a clean enough loop for the signals and operators to make sense.

**Subjective face**

Clarity, reduced confusion-noise, stronger sense that the parts fit together.

**Main operator anchors**

- Y4 Map
- Y11 Meta-map
- Y12 Evaluate
- Y16 Level control

**Typical failure modes**

- conceptual muddle
- incompatible goals
- unstable standards
- noisy self-monitoring

### Z3.3 Compression depth | Gist quality

**Definition**

The ability to extract the deeper invariant, relation, or organising rule rather than merely collecting surface outputs.

**Why it matters**

The G-Loop is explicitly a selective compression loop, and the formal-layer appendix makes clear that gist extraction and insight arise procedurally through mapping, chunking, abstraction, re-representation, meta-mapping, and later testing. Good H-AGI use therefore depends on whether the human is extracting real structure or merely borrowing polished phrasing. Good compression also depends on being able to build, revise, and stabilise temporary, arbitrary, novel bindings rather than merely repeat familiar associations.

**Subjective face**

Relief, coherence, a felt click, clarity, excitement, confidence. These may be useful markers of compression success, but they are not self-validating.

**Main operator anchors**

- Y4 Map
- Y5 Chunk
- Y8 Abstract
- Y10 Re-represent
- Y11 Meta-map
- Y12–Y15 for pressure-testing and commitment

**Typical failure modes**

- answer collection
- borrowed fluency
- virtual gist
- attractive but shallow compression

### Z3.4 Complexity governance | Map cost vs revision cost

**Definition**

The ability to distinguish between:

- **map cost**: the current model is too heavy to hold and run cleanly
- **revision cost**: one’s own understanding, policy, or skill structure needs restructuring

**Why it matters**

The updated material makes this distinction explicit. Complexity in the signal board is the burden of the current map, while mismatch and unexpected mismatch drive the cost of revision. Good intelligence must know when to simplify and when to tolerate the harder work of restructuring. Map cost is not only about having many parts. It is also about the burden of keeping bound relational structure coherent enough to manipulate online.

**Subjective face**

- **Map cost**: clutter, overload, too many moving parts, cognitive fatigue
- **Revision cost**: friction, disconfirmation, “something is off”, the unpleasantness of giving up a frame that no longer fits

**Main operator anchors**

- Y4 Map
- Y5 Chunk
- Y6 Unpack
- Y10 Re-represent
- Y12 Evaluate
- Y16 Level control

**Typical failure modes**

- over-simplifying when revision is needed
- persevering in needless complexity when simplification is the right move

### Z3.5 Subjective calibration | Feeling as signal, not verdict

**Definition**

The ability to read feelings, urges, pressure, relief, and excitement as possible indicators of loop conditions without mistaking them for proof.

**Why it matters**

Trident-G already treats subjectivity as the lived interface of the loop and the signal board. Pressure may reflect load, urgency may reflect salience, felt “offness” may track mismatch, and relief may accompany compression success. But these cues are signal-rich and fallible.

**Subjective face**

This dial is itself about subjectivity: noticing what the feeling might be tracking.

**Main operator anchors**

- Y1 Orient
- Y2 Recover
- Y10 Re-represent
- Y12 Evaluate
- Y13 Discriminate / Probe

**Typical failure modes**

- trusting feelings as verdicts
- ignoring useful signals
- mistaking relief for truth
- mistaking urgency for importance

### Z3.6 Proof discipline | Validation severity

**Definition**

The strength of the system’s insistence on probes, boundary checks, wrapper variation, and real-world confirmation before banking something as competence.

**Why it matters**

The far-transfer protocol is explicit: portability is not assumed, validation must precede banking, local plausibility is insufficient, and real or strongly mission-proximal success outranks local fluency. In the AI age this becomes even more central because plausible-looking outputs are abundant.

**Subjective face**

Healthy scepticism, curiosity about falsification, resistance to premature closure, less seduction by coherence alone.

**Main operator anchors**

- Y12 Evaluate
- Y13 Discriminate / Probe
- Y14 Validate portability

**Typical failure modes**

- thin automation
- banking plausibility
- mistaking elegance for truth
- treating one clean thread as proof of generality

### Z3.7 Progress–quality governance | Satisficing versus perfectionism

**Definition**

The ability to balance forward movement through the problem space against adequate local rigour at each step.

**Why it matters**

The kernel explicitly says the loop must stop hovering. Y15 is the discipline of committing once the signal is good enough for the current rigour budget, while salience governs how much loop is warranted. This means good intelligence is not maximal local perfection at every step, nor sloppy speed. It is calibrated progress.

**Subjective face**

- **Over-perfectionism**: tension, hesitation, inability to close, local over-processing
- **Under-rigour**: impulsive relief, premature certainty, shallow progress

**Main operator anchors**

- Y1 Orient
- Y13 Discriminate / Probe
- Y15 Satisfice / Commit
- Y16 Level control

**Typical failure modes**

- hovering
- endless refinement
- premature closure
- local polishing that blocks strategic movement

### Z3.8 Exploration–exploitation governance | Strategic mode balance

**Definition**

The higher-order ability to judge when to keep extracting affordances from the current map, script, or strategy, and when to widen, restructure, or reconfigure.

**Why it matters**

This is already latent in the Trident structure itself: the outer tines are exploration and exploitation, the centre mediates movement between them, and the control missions already include Calibrate mode and Arbitrate improvisation vs script. But this choice is too important to remain only implicit. It needs to be a trainable meta-dial.

**Subjective face**

- **Over-exploitation**: closure, comfort, local confidence, hidden brittleness
- **Over-exploration**: restlessness, novelty pull, repeated reopening, difficulty consolidating
- **Good balance**: enough openness to revise, enough closure to exploit the current affordance surface

**Main operator anchors**

**Exploration side**
- Y4 Map
- Y7 Analogise
- Y10 Re-represent
- Y11 Meta-map
- Y13 Discriminate / Probe

**Exploitation side**
- Y8 Abstract
- Y9 Instantiate
- Y12 Evaluate
- Y14 Validate portability
- Y15 Satisfice / Commit

**Typical failure modes**

- clinging to a stale strategy
- repeatedly abandoning workable positions before extracting their value

### Z3.9 Multi-scale governance | Horizon and granularity control

**Definition**

The higher-order ability to allocate cognition appropriately across scales of time, abstraction, and task granularity, so that the G-Loop moves cleanly between strategic framing, problem-space navigation, and detailed execution without getting trapped at the wrong level.

**Why it matters**

This competence is partly built into the existing architecture, especially through level control, mapping, chunking, unpacking, re-representation, and meta-mapping. But it should not remain only implicit at the operator level. In real environments, many failures come not from weak reasoning within a chosen frame, but from running the loop at the wrong scale altogether: too much big-picture reflection when concrete execution is needed, too much local technical optimisation when strategic steering is required, or too much day-level task churn without reference to the wider mission. For that reason, multi-scale control is best treated as a trainable meta-dial for overall problem-space and G-Loop management across time, space, and cognitive niche.

**Subjective face**

Poor scale fit often feels like busyness without traction, pressure without proportion, fragmentation across too many levels at once, or getting lost either in detail or in vague strategy. Good scale fit often feels like cleaner traction, better proportion, smoother movement between planning and doing, and a stronger sense that the present level of thought matches the real demands of the task.

**Main operator anchors**

- Y1 Orient for rigour and scale budgeting
- Y4 Map for structuring the problem space
- Y5 Chunk and Y6 Unpack for moving between packaged and detailed structure
- Y10 Re-represent and Y11 Meta-map for shifting level and frame while preserving what matters
- Y15 Satisfice / Commit for converting the right level of understanding into action
- Y16 Level control / anti-regress as the main local operator anchor for granularity regulation

**Typical failure modes**

- over-abstraction, where the person remains at the level of strategy, theory, or vision without translating this into executable next moves
- over-localisation, where the person optimises detail while neglecting the broader mission frame
- unstable switching between scales without clean integration
- poor day-level task selection, where what is worked on bears too little relation to the current strategic position or the most valuable next constraint to address

### Z3.10 Ecology support | Friction and niche fit

**Definition**

The degree to which environment, workflow, tools, schedule, and broader niche make good loops easier or harder to repeat.

**Why it matters**

Many failures of intelligent action are ecological before they are inferential. Good training should therefore include workspace synergies, routine design, and niche fit rather than treating cognition as detached from context.

**Subjective face**

Lower friction, lower dread, easier re-entry, more repeatable clean starts.

**Main operator anchors**

Indirect rather than local, especially Y3 Externalise / Escalate and the control-mission family.

**Typical failure modes**

Good reasoning in theory, low repeatability in life.

## Z4. Why these belong in the meta-dial layer

These competences should not be added as extra Y-operators.

The formal-layer appendix is already clear that the operator set should remain compact, and that things such as explore versus exploit belong elsewhere in the architecture rather than being added as extra primitives. The operator algebra is the concrete move-language of the kernel, not the full training logic.

These meta-dials are therefore best treated as:

- distributed over operators
- visible in subjective and behavioural patterns
- measurable in telemetry over repeated loops
- trainable as organising principles

That makes them ideal for programmes, coaching, and app design.

## Z5. Training implications

The main practical implication is that Trident-G training should not only teach:

- mission frames
- Learn as an acquisition mode
- control missions
- operators
- tactics
- strategies
- reasoning styles
- portability checks

It should also teach users to recognise and train the higher-order geometry of good looping.

So a programme may foreground:

- compression depth in an insight or comprehension block
- complexity governance in an LLM-assisted learning block
- proof discipline in a decision or argument audit block
- progress–quality governance in strategic planning
- exploration–exploitation governance in business, research, and mission design
- subjective calibration in resilience and H-AGI use
- meta-efficacy and ecology support in adherence and consistency work

This fits the scaling principle that products can vary in foreground emphasis while preserving one shared kernel and one shared formal architecture.

## Z6. Telemetry implications

Each meta-dial should also have associated telemetry.

### Compression depth
- invariant restatement quality
- cross-syntax restatement
- wrapper-robust gist retention

### Complexity governance
- correct identification of map cost versus revision cost
- simplification timing
- restructuring persistence when needed

### Subjective calibration
- accuracy of signal interpretation
- reduced confusion between relief and truth
- better alignment between felt and actual loop state

### Proof discipline
- probe frequency
- boundary checking
- wrapper-swap pass rate
- real-world validation rate

### Progress–quality governance
- hover time before commit
- premature closure rate
- completion quality under time constraints

### Exploration–exploitation governance
- premature pivot rate
- stale persistence rate
- affordance extraction before reconfiguration
- strategic pivot quality

This makes the meta-dial layer especially suitable for training apps.

## Z7. Canonical summary

Trident-G should include a clearer user-facing meta-level architecture in which two meta-missions sit above the meta-dial layer: **Viability**, the maintenance of stable, recoverable, in-band functioning, and **Expansion**, the enlargement of viable dynamic range and banked portable competence. These meta-missions should be treated as trainable global orientations, not as peer meta-dials. Beneath them sits a clearer meta-dial layer that serves as the main organising layer for training, coaching, and telemetry. This layer does not replace the G-Loop kernel, the mission framework, or the formal layer. It shapes how well those layers are run across time and across contexts. The canonical meta-dials are: meta-efficacy, coherence, compression depth, complexity governance, subjective calibration, proof discipline, progress–quality governance, exploration–exploitation governance, multi-scale governance, and ecology support. These are slower shapers of cognitive geometry rather than fast in-session moves. They recruit multiple operators, appear in distinctive subjective and behavioural patterns, and can therefore be trained directly as organising principles for H-AGI and Trident-G development.
