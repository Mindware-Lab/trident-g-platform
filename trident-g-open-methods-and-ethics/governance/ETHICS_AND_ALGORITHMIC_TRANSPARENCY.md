# ETHICS_AND_ALGORITHMIC_TRANSPARENCY.md

## 1) Purpose and limits

These methods are for training, research, and educational use.
They are not medical diagnosis or treatment systems.

## 2) Human agency commitments

- No dark patterns in onboarding, retention, or pricing flows
- No coercive recommendation loops
- No hidden gating that silently removes user rights
- Recommendations are advisory, not compulsory
- Users retain control over export and participation settings

## 3) Input category transparency

At method level we disclose input categories, for example:
- readiness/state categories
- trend and quality signals
- probe/re-check outcomes
- baseline-relative comparisons

## 4) Output category transparency

At method level we disclose output categories, for example:
- session/style recommendations
- next-step guidance
- confidence/caveat labels
- transfer-check/evidence statuses

## 5) Conservative defaults and risk controls

- Conservative behavior under uncertainty
- Explicit invalid/low-quality routing
- Recovery-first routing for unstable runs
- Versioned changelogs with rollback posture

## 6) Claims and accountability

- Keep claims aligned to non-clinical boundaries
- Keep evidence status explicit and module-specific
- Publish caveats and update cadence for aggregate summaries

## 7) Privacy and publication boundaries

- No user-identifiable data publication
- No identifiable clinical data accepted via public channels
- Aggregate-only publication with suppression policy controls
