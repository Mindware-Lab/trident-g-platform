Section 00 - Stewardship

Scientific stewardship and protocol leadership

IQMindware protocols are developed and maintained by Dr Mark Ashton Smith under the Trident G computational framework, with public protocol and data surfaces.

Scientific lead

Dr Mark Ashton Smith is a cognitive neuroscientist trained at the Center for the Neural Basis of Cognition (University of Pittsburgh and Carnegie Mellon University), former lecturer and researcher at the University of Cambridge, and former Lecturer in Cognitive Psychology and Neuroscience at the University of Essex Online.

Method and boundaries

Protocols are designed to test transfer under changed conditions using game swaps, boundary and trap probes, and delayed re-checks. This is skills training and self-regulation, not diagnosis or treatment.

Section 01 - Claims

What we can and cannot claim

What we say is governed by a claims ladder contract and testimonial constraints.

claims-ladder.json
✓
Allowed claims
  • ✓Designed to train general intelligence capacity and cognitive resilience.
  • ✓We test transfer under game swaps (same skill in a different task format), boundary/trap probes (failure-point checks), and delayed re-checks.
  • ✓We track trends and publish aggregated summaries as evidence grows.
  • ✓Skills training and self-regulation, not diagnosis or treatment.
✕
Not allowed
  • ✕Guaranteed far transfer or guaranteed outcomes.
  • ✕Clinical-proof language without specific cited evidence.
  • ✕Diagnosis, treatment, or cure framing.
  • ✕Universal outcome claims from testimonials.
⚠
Boundary: IQMindware products are performance and self-regulation tools. They are not medical devices and not intended for diagnosis or treatment. Outcomes vary.
