Programme and how it works

The basic structure of Trident G IQ Pro and the first-session path.

Trident G IQ Pro is one integrated app with four modules: Zone Pulse, Capacity Gym, Reasoning Gym, and Tracker. It includes the structured 20-day Coach-led in-app programme plus Manual mode for self-directed training.

Start on Start here. The normal route is: take the Tracker baseline, use Zone Pulse before each training session, then complete Capacity Gym and Reasoning Gym targets in Coach-led mode.

Zone Pulse takes about 3 minutes. A combined Capacity Gym plus Reasoning Gym Coach-led session is usually about 20 to 30 minutes, depending on the route and dose set by the app.

The system is designed to build general cognitive efficiency in attention control, working memory, and reasoning quality, then verify carryover under changed conditions.

  • Stay effective under distraction and interruption.
  • Use AI without eroding independent reasoning.
  • Prevent throughput burnout from constant acceleration.
  • Build cognitive reliability across demanding days.
  • Make better decisions under uncertainty and time pressure.

Far transfer and what it means

What the programme is designed to test beyond one familiar game.

Far transfer means carry-over under changed conditions, not just getting faster inside one trained game. IQMindware uses wrapper swaps, boundary probes, delayed re-checks, and pre/post measures to test whether gains travel beyond the training surface.

Practice effects usually improve performance inside a familiar task wrapper. Trident G IQ Pro is designed against that by combining readiness gating, horizontal swaps, vertical progression, and delayed checks on objective measures.

Use AI after you map your own answer first. IQMindware focuses on readiness gating, script quality, and transfer checks so AI acts as augmentation, not a replacement for independent reasoning.

3

Zone Pulse, Capacity, Reasoning, and Tracker

What each module does inside the programme.

Zone Pulse uses the masked Multiple Function Task, or MFT-m, as a daily readiness and cognitive control capacity check. The output helps set the day's training route and dose.

Capacity Gym trains working memory and attention control through adaptive N-back and relational N-back tasks. It uses wrapper swaps, load progression, and speed adjustment to build run-time control and portability.

Reasoning Gym trains relation matching, constraint solving, and must-follow inference with meaning and surface swaps. It is the vertical transfer layer of the programme.

Tracker provides baseline, post-training, and interval progress checks. It includes brief objective reasoning measures and applied intelligence checks for evidence, not training credit.

4

Coach-led vs Manual mode

How the app decides whether a session advances the core programme.

Coach-led in-app mode runs the structured 20-day programme. Zone Pulse sets the route, and the session count only advances when both Capacity Gym and Reasoning Gym targets are complete.

Support routes are real training, but they do not advance the core 20-session count. They are used to stabilise, reduce load, or rebuild control when Zone Pulse says that is the better route.

Manual mode lets you explore modules directly for review, curiosity, and self-directed practice. Coach-led mode is recommended when you want the structured far-transfer programme.
