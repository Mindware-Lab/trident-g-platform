# IQ Mindware Substack Series Outline
**Version:** v1  
**Project:** IQ Mindware / Trident G / H-AGI  
**Purpose:** Repo-ready outline for a free + paid Substack series that combines:
- transferable intelligence and proof under change
- the H-AGI human-led AI reasoning loop
- practical workstation-stack building
- Trident G app and protocol integration

---

## 1. Editorial frame

### Umbrella
**IQ Mindware** stays the centre.  
**Trident G** is the deeper method.  
**H-AGI** is the human-led AI add-on rail.

### Core promise
**Train general intelligence capacity and portable thinking skills — then prove they carry over under changed conditions.**

### AI-facing version
**Build a human-led AI workstation for real life and work — then use it without surrendering reasoning, judgement, or cognitive ownership.**

### Core publishing split
- **Free posts** build trust, explain the world, introduce the mechanisms, and warm readers toward the paid layer.
- **Paid posts** operationalise the framework into workstation builds, practical workflows, reusable scripts, and proof-guided use.

---

## 2. Series logic

The series has two linked aims:

### A. Build the workstation
Show readers how to assemble and use a practical human-led AI stack for:
- research
- writing
- email
- planning
- travel
- personal finance review
- weekly review
- decision-making
- light agentic workflows

### B. Protect and train the thinker
Show readers how to use that workstation through the **H-AGI Loop**:

**Zone -> Own -> Refine -> Stress-test -> Audit -> Re-write / Bank -> Reapply -> Proof**

This means:
- preserving first-pass human framing
- sharpening deep relational mapping
- extracting reusable mindware scripts
- reapplying them under changed conditions
- checking what actually carries over

---

## 3. Free rail

## Public Lab / Transfer Under Change
**Purpose:** attract, educate, interpret, and build trust.

### Editorial job
Free posts should:
- explain the IQ Mindware world clearly
- introduce transfer, Zone, capacity, mindware, and G-Loop
- show the proof-first posture
- make readers want the practical implementation layer
- stay readable, grounded, and non-pushy

### Recurring free structure
Each free post should usually include:
1. one live problem
2. one mechanism
3. one proof cue
4. one light CTA

### Typical free CTA
- join Product Updates
- start the Transfer Sprint
- subscribe for the paid field manual
- try a short reflection or logging exercise

---

## 4. Paid rail

## H-AGI Workbench
**Purpose:** build the workstation, protect the thinker.

### Editorial job
Paid posts should:
- teach the workstation stack and workflows
- teach the H-AGI Loop and graded engagement
- show how to use AI without giving away judgement
- connect back to Zone, Gf -> Gc -> Gf, mindware scripts, and proof checks
- provide templates, scripts, worksheets, or checklists

### Recurring paid structure
Each paid post should usually include:
1. what we are building this week
2. what task it is for
3. what part of the H-AGI Loop it uses
4. what script or operator might be extracted
5. what proof or re-check will show whether it carries over
6. one practical asset

### Typical paid CTA
- use the worksheet
- run the one-week challenge
- try the workflow and log results
- join Practice Club / Live Cohort Pass lightly when relevant

## 7. Strong editorial rules

Every free post should cash out into at least one of:
- transfer under change
- state and capacity
- mindware scripts
- G-Loop
- proof posture

Every paid post should cash out into at least one of:
- a workstation build step
- a human-led reasoning protocol step
- a reusable script
- a proof or re-check rule

The publication should never drift into:
- generic AI news
- generic productivity content
- prompt-hack culture detached from judgement
- vague transhuman rhetoric
- app promotion without explanation
